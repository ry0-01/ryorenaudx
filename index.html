Renesme’s Case

Sunday, 5:30am. A janitor was about to start his cleaning routine when he suddenly noticed blood coming from the 5th floor. He rushed upstairs and discovered the victim lying on the staircase. He attempted to help, but the victim was already stiff (early rigor mortis) and unresponsive. He immediately called the police.

The victim was later identified as Renesme Sultan, an intern at the IDR department.

crime scene details

  • body was found on the stairs

Letter from Paluerza

Ten years ago, Romano Aleksandro, aged 73, was found guilty of being the serial killer behind a string of murders that terrorized the village of Paluerza. The verdict shocked the entire community. Despite his kind demeanor and gentle appearance, Romano was revealed to be the mastermind behind the killings that had plagued the village for years.

present day

A group of workers was assigned to clean and renovate an old house that once belonged to Don Romano Aleksandro. As they were removing furniture from the property, one of the workers attempted to lift a red sofa. Suddenly, an envelope slipped from beneath it and fell to the floor.

Romano's name remained infamous throughout Paluerza. Even after a decade, people still speculated about the reasons behind his crimes. Unfortunately, those questions were never answered. Only a few days after his arrest, Romano passed away, taking his secrets with him.

additional information

  • Romano Aleksandro died only days after his conviction
  • no motive was ever officially established
  • his grandson, Caesar Aleksandro, disappeared from public view shortly afterward
  • local rumors claim Caesar was taken to South Carolina by relatives
  • the discovered envelope was immediately surrendered to authorities

recovered document

Inside the envelope was a single sheet of paper believed to have been written by Romano himself.

Investigators could not immediately determine whether it was truly a letter, as the page contained what appeared to be a series of random letters and symbols rather than a coherent message. Despite this, authorities believed the contents were intentional and likely concealed a deeper meaning.
